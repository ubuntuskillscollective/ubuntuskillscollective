About Ubuntu Skills Collective

Our Mission

At Ubuntu Skills Collective, our mission is to empower South African youth by bridging the skills gap and equipping them with the tools and training needed to succeed in high-demand sectors. By transforming challenges into opportunities, we drive economic growth and build sustainable futures for communities.

What We Do

  • Youth Unemployment: Creating pathways into meaningful employment for unemployed or underemployed youth.
  • Skills Mismatch: Addressing shortages in key industries like renewable energy, technology, and trades by aligning training programs with market demands.

Strategic Partnership Opportunity

South Africa faces a dual crisis: youth unemployment and a critical skills mismatch that stifles economic growth. At Ubuntu Skills Collective, we are creating a pipeline of job-ready talent in high-demand sectors such as renewable energy, tech, and trades. We invite your organization to join us in this transformative mission.

Why We Are Doing This

Youth unemployment is not just a statistic—it’s a systemic failure. Over 3.4 million young South Africans are neither employed nor in education, while industries like renewable energy and IT struggle to fill roles. For example, the renewable energy sector requires 26,000 skilled workers by 2030, yet training programs remain inaccessible to marginalized communities. Our data-driven insights, combined with free, in-person training campuses, are turning unemployed youth into employable assets.

What We Aim to Achieve

  1. Train 1,000 youth annually in certifications aligned with South Africa’s Critical Skills List (e.g., solar installation, coding, AI basics).
  2. Place 70% of graduates into jobs, internships, or gig projects within 6 months.
  3. Reduce dependency on social grants by building a skilled workforce contributing R500M annually to the economy by 2027.

Why Partner with Us?

Your impact will be measurable:

  • B-BBEE Compliance: Earn points via Skills Development contributions (SETA-accredited programs).
  • ESG Alignment: Support UN SDG 8 (Decent Work) and South Africa’s National Development Plan.
  • Workforce Pipeline: Access pre-trained talent in high-demand sectors.
